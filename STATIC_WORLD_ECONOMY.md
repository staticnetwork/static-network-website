# STATIC World Economy Canon

## Core Hook

**Buy your land. Build your world. Welcome to STATIC.**

STATIC City is the shared core. Everyone returns there to shop, create, socialize,
compete, broadcast, discover, and access the districts. Around the core city are
The Fields: massive purchasable terrain zones where players expand outward by
buying land, building on it, defending it, trading assets, or taking territory
through approved game systems.

## World Layout

1. **STATIC City**
   - The authored center of the world.
   - STATIC City sits on a fully water-surrounded island core. The city feels
     protected, cinematic, and central: a glowing capital in the middle of a
     massive world.
   - Bridges, causeways, tunnels, and future ground transit connect the city
     island only to the three mainland Fields regions. Ferries, water taxis,
     boats, yachts, aircraft, and future air routes connect to island locations.
   - Contains the core districts: Arrival, Signals, Signal Walk, Market Walk,
     Radio Rooftop, STATIC PLAY, Wondercore, Boardwalk, Marina, Solar Coast,
     Velocity, Rootside, Rise, Sky Crown, Arena, Island, and future venues.
   - Players cannot simply hide behind private land. They must return to the
     core city to buy assets, create district-specific items, attend events,
     access systems, and interact with the public economy.
   - Beneath the core is a hidden tunnel network: service corridors,
     maintenance lines, utility shafts, freight routes, abandoned transit
     arteries, Rootside shortcuts, venue backdoors, and underground checkpoints.
     Tunnels create stealth, courier, smuggling-fiction, repair, rescue, chase,
     shortcut, and hidden-clue gameplay without replacing public streets.

2. **The Fields**
   - Thousands of acres of surrounding terrain parcels.
   - Mostly empty at first by design. STATIC provides the world foundation,
     terrain, rules, assets, and tools; players build the outer world.
   - The Fields are not just suburbs or side lots. They are the macro-world
     layer where almost anything can happen inside the rules of STATIC: private
     homes, clubs, theme parks, creator cities, ranches, faction lands, art
     camps, festivals, custom arenas, retreats, hidden compounds, beach worlds,
     snow fortresses, forest towns, desert convoys, and player-built kingdoms.
   - Scale should not be artificially capped at neighborhood size. A single
     player, crew, company, celebrity, creator collective, or faction can
     eventually control land at the scale of an entire temporary festival city,
     a large ranch, or even national-park-sized territory if the economy,
     server architecture, and rules support it.
   - Ownership can be huge, but rendering should be streamed. The database can
     remember continent-scale ownership while the engine loads only nearby
     cells, districts, interiors, events, and active conflict zones.
   - The Fields are divided into three primary open-land mega-regions so the
     world feels legible at map scale: Desert Region, Forest Region, and Snow
     Region.
   - These are the only open-land mainland regions directly connected to STATIC
     City by bridge/causeway/tunnel.
   - Other terrain fantasies such as ranch land, wasteland, farmland, beaches,
     mountains, hidden compounds, and festival cities live inside one of the
     three mega-regions instead of becoming separate mainland landmasses.
   - Regions can be continent-scale. The Desert Region should feel Sahara-scale,
     the Forest Region should feel Amazon-scale, and the Snow Region should
     feel Nepal/Everest-scale with extreme verticality, mountain passes, and
     high-altitude danger. These are scale references, not copied geographies.
   - Region scale and property scale are separate. A region can be vast enough
     to contain thousands or millions of properties; an individual property or
     controlled estate still caps at Yellowstone-scale unless the rule changes
     later.
   - Terrain matters strategically: forests provide cover and survival paths,
     deserts support outlaw/Mad-Max-style builds and convoy routes, snow
     regions support faction conflict and fortress warfare. Ranches, farms,
     communes, rodeos, retreats, and private compounds can exist within those
     mega-regions depending on terrain pockets and player builds.
   - Macro-map inspiration should come from territory strategy games only at
     the structural level: parcels, resource value, terrain advantages, travel
     routes, faction borders, contested windows, and large-scale ownership.
     Do not copy any specific game's map, UI, names, symbols, or seasonal reset
     design.

3. **STATIC Island Side**
   - The right/eastern side of the world map should read primarily as water.
   - A single large STATIC Island sits in the center of that water as the major
     island destination.
   - There is no bridge from STATIC City to STATIC Island. Island access is by
     sea or air only: yachts, boats, ferries, water taxis, helicopters,
     seaplanes, aircraft, and future premium transit.
   - Smaller docks, marina routes, private-island access lanes, and founder
     estate routes can exist, but they should not create a second mainland.
   - STATIC Island is both a luxury vacation destination and the highest-density
     rare-character discovery zone. The resort shore stays social and
     welcoming; the deeper rings become progressively harder, rarer, and more
     mythic.
   - Island rings: Resort Shore, Character Wild Belt, Frontline Perimeter,
     Fortress Wall, Moat And Inner Grounds, and Peak Compound.
   - Character acquisition on the island must be opt-in and designed through
     challenge, stealth, rescue, negotiation, respect, treaty, surrender, or
     story outcomes. It is not random griefing and it does not allow players to
     seize protected founder property.

4. **El Mirage**
   - Working name for a year-round desert festival destination inside The
     Fields desert region. Final name should receive clearance because similar
     real-world place names exist.
   - El Mirage is a permanent STATIC desert culture hub: giant entrance gate,
     security checkpoints, art roads, camp grids, mutant vehicles, light
     towers, dust trails, night lasers, sunrise stages, maker camps, pop-up
     lounges, and massive rave/music-festival atmosphere.
   - The district teaches players the festival-building language. Visitors can
     chill, attend desert raves, meet builders, join camp quests, buy assets,
     earn blueprints, and create their own festival grounds.
   - Creation loop: players use El Mirage build stations to generate festival
     assets such as stages, speaker towers, LED sculptures, camp gates, art
     cars, tents, shade structures, neon towers, projection walls, vendor
     booths, dance floors, fire-safe visual effects, and crowd props. They can
     bring those assets back to owned land in The Fields.
   - El Mirage scale can range from a single player's festival parcel to a
     Burning-Man-sized creator city or larger. The core idea is persistent
     self-expression, not temporary destruction.

5. **Origin Settlements**
   - The Fields should contain system-owned origin settlements that explain
     why unusual assets exist in STATIC: fantasy species, robots, guardians,
     mounts, creatures, performers, vendors, bodyguards, machine clans, desert
     caravans, forest settlements, snow citadels, island colonies, and hidden
     tunnel communities.
   - Origin settlements are not real-world ethnic groups or colonial targets.
     They are fictional STATIC cultures, factions, ecologies, labs, clans,
     creature habitats, and machine societies with their own rules.
   - Players can interact through diplomacy, trade, rescue, hiring, quests,
     reputation, treaties, creator drops, settlement markets, and opt-in
     conflict windows.
   - Settlement citizens or units can join a player-built city only through
     invitation, hiring, alliance, citizenship, migration, quest outcomes, or
     story rules. Avoid forced-assimilation framing.
   - Protected origin hubs cannot be randomly destroyed or farmed. Conflict
     versions should be instanced, moderated, and rule-bound.
   - Each settlement can introduce region-specific asset families into the
     economy: buildings, decor, clothing, vehicles, food, companion families,
     mounts, guard types, tools, rituals, festival kits, and blueprints.
   - Every new character, creature, guard, vendor, companion, or unusual
     humanoid asset pack must be assigned to a home region, district, faction,
     settlement, or island ring before it enters the live world. If placement is
     unclear, keep it in catalog/quarantine status until the owner approves the
     correct home.
   - Once roughly 10-20 compatible assets exist in one settlement family, they
     can begin forming autonomous NPC ecosystems: crews, courts, gangs, clans,
     villages, labs, cults, machine societies, orders, colonies, or factions.
     These groups can slowly develop routines, trade, rivalries, leadership,
     resource needs, relationships, celebrations, migrations, conflicts, and
     territory goals.
   - Valuable land can require **claim pressure** before building opens. Claim
     pressure means the parcel has local hazards, guardians, factions, ruins,
     settlement politics, or survival threats that must be resolved first.
   - Forest parcels can be guarded by humanoid warrior-animal clans, creature
     patrols, treehouse settlements, scouts, and forest guardians. Players can
     clear hostile patrols, negotiate treaties, rescue settlement members, or
     earn surrender/alliance outcomes.
   - Snow parcels can be overrun by fictional zombies/infected, survival waves,
     icebound ruins, and extreme-weather hazards. If not cleared, attacks keep
     pressuring the build site.
   - Desert claim pressure is still open for design. Candidate lanes include
     dust raiders, machine scavengers, sand megafauna, rogue convoys, mirage
     cults, weather trials, or desert spirits.
   - Stronger parcels can require stronger gear, companions, allies,
     reputation, Signal Credits, or quest completion before ownership is safe.
   - Recruited or surrendered NPCs become allies, guards, citizens, workers,
     scouts, or story followers through stateful rules. They should not be
     treated as farmed commodities.
   - Major origin chains can culminate in an oversized boss/champion. Resolving
     that boss can trigger settlement surrender, treaty access, rare blueprints,
     recruitment permissions, boss-companion variants, banners, cosmetics,
     land-defense units, or region reputation.
   - Boss companions should be rare prestige unlocks with district limits,
     cooldowns, mode restrictions, and balance rules. They should not become
     permanent pay-to-win public-space weapons.

6. **STATIC Universe**
   - STATIC Universe is the late-game expansion above STATIC City and The
     Fields. It should not distract from shipping the core city first.
   - Players eventually reach the orbital layer through STATIC Astral Port,
     cinematic launch routes, starship hangars, and STATIC Orbital Station.
   - The orbital station is the final Earth-side mission hub and universe
     unlock point. The unlock beat is: **Prepare for takeoff. STATIC Universe
     unlocked.**
   - Planet Genesis is the next scale jump. Instead of "buy land, build your
     world," qualified players can eventually design a planet: biomes,
     regions, cities, oceans, defenses, settlements, resources, trade routes,
     and visitor rules.
   - Planet ownership is game-state ownership only. It does not represent
     real-world land, cash-value property, or legal ownership.
   - Solar-system gameplay can include trade, resource routes, planet defense,
     alliances, visitors, orbital events, off-world settlements, alien
     diplomacy, and opt-in planet-vs-planet conflict windows.
   - Conflict must be moderated and rule-bound. No permanent griefing, no
     pay-to-win planet destruction, no real-cash wagers, and no unreviewed
     cash-value rewards.

## Scale References

STATIC should use real-world scale references to keep the ambition honest.

- Burning Man / Black Rock City proves that one temporary, participant-built
  culture can occupy multiple square miles and support tens of thousands of
  people through camps, art, infrastructure, roads, rituals, and shared rules.
  STATIC should adapt the spirit of radical self-expression, participation,
  communal effort, and self-reliance, but make the result persistent instead of
  temporary. The STATIC version does not burn down or wipe the world at the end.
- A Burning-Man-scale build should be possible as one player's land, one crew's
  event ground, one creator's festival, or one faction's temporary capital.
  This is the expected aspirational scale for serious builders, not a weird
  edge case.
- Yellowstone-scale ownership is the hard per-property ceiling. The National
  Park Service lists Yellowstone at 2.2 million acres, which is the maximum
  magnitude a single property, estate, company campus, founder zone, faction
  territory, or player-controlled landmass should be allowed to reach.
- Region-scale terrain can be far larger than any one property. A single
  Desert Region can be Sahara-scale, a single Forest Region can be
  Amazon-scale, and a single Snow Region can be Nepal/Everest-scale while still
  containing many individually owned, contested, public, protected, or
  system-owned parcels.
- The player should feel no small fantasy ceiling, but the system needs a
  defined upper bound. The technical ceiling is handled through world
  partitioning, procedural generation, streaming cells, LOD, instancing, and
  server authority.

## Starter Land

- A new player should eventually be able to start with a low-cost starter plot
  conceptually priced around **$0.99**, subject to final payment, platform, tax,
  fraud, age, and legal review.
- The starter plot should be generous enough for a small house, yard, basic
  build items, and a reason to care.
- The starter package can include a deliberately rough starter kit: a small
  plot, a basic shack or trailer, minimal furniture, a cheap vehicle or travel
  voucher, a starter defensive tool/loadout, and a tiny Signal Credit balance.
  The message is clear: go into STATIC City and build a better life, or take a
  riskier outlaw path and try to earn faster through missions, theft systems,
  faction work, bounties, territory conflict, or heist-style gameplay.
- The starter weapon fantasy must be game-bound, age-gated where needed, and
  rule-limited. It should not enable random griefing, real-world instructions,
  protected-zone attacks, or harassment of new players. It belongs inside
  missions, contested zones, opt-in conflict, self-defense, faction systems, and
  moderated gameplay loops.
- More land can be purchased through the STATIC Store, earned through play,
  leased/traded from other players, or taken through approved opt-in conflict
  systems.

## Player Cities And Border Names

Once a player, crew, company, faction, or creator collective controls enough
contiguous land, they can name the settlement, camp, estate, district, city, or
territory they are building.

- Names appear around the controlled border on maps, region overlays, signage,
  travel routes, and future aerial views.
- Naming is earned by land scale, continuity, activity, and reputation, not just
  by buying one tiny lot.
- Names must pass moderation, trademark, impersonation, hate/harassment, and
  profanity checks.
- The same region can contain many named player places: desert festival cities,
  forest compounds, snow fortresses, beach towns, ranch empires, private islands,
  mountain estates, and wasteland outposts.
- Region identity still matters. A city in the Desert Region should feel
  different from a city in the Forest Region, Snow Region, Beach/Coast Region,
  or Ranch Region.

## Persistence Rule

STATIC should not reset player-owned worlds every season.

- Players keep land, builds, and earned ownership persistently.
- Seasons can add new events, resources, risks, rewards, cosmetics, and conflict
  windows, but should not wipe legitimate owned property by default.
- Land can change hands only through clearly defined systems: sale, trade,
  lease, owner transfer, foreclosure-style inactive rules if ever adopted,
  faction conquest, legal in-world seizure, or opt-in property conflict.

## Build Economy

Players buy, unlock, craft, generate, trade, or earn assets, then place them on
land they control.

Examples:

- houses, cabins, mansions, castles, apartments, compounds
- garages, barns, bunkers, stores, clubs, stages, arenas
- furniture, decor, lights, cameras, speakers, instruments
- vehicles, vehicle parts, wraps, rims, paint, underglow
- clothing, jewelry, skins, bags, shoes, accessories
- rides, attraction modules, escape rooms, stunt arenas
- farms, crops, ranch assets, animals, gates, fences
- security, walls, turrets, drones, guards, traps, cameras
- roads, bridges, paths, trails, docks, airstrips
- event props, festival gear, booths, food stands, screens

STATIC can ship monthly original asset drops that expand what players can build.
These are not copies of real games, films, brands, or weapon systems; they are
STATIC-native packs inspired by categories of play:

- vehicle packs: street cars, luxury SUVs, bikes, trucks, boats, jet skis,
  aircraft, wraps, rims, tuning parts, underglow, garage tools
- action packs: fictional weapons, armor, shields, defense props, training
  ranges, faction gear, security assets
- land packs: desert, forest, snow, island, ranch, suburban, skyline, fortress,
  marina, festival, boardwalk, and nightlife build sets
- venue packs: club interiors, stages, screens, bars, rides, arenas, shops,
  hotel/casino modules, galleries, studios, creator booths
- companion packs: guards, robots, fantasy companions, pets, mounts, drones,
  and land-defense NPC shells
- origin-settlement packs: fictional settlement kits, creature nests, machine
  yards, desert caravans, forest clan builds, snow citadels, island colonies,
  tunnel camps, guards, vendors, and settlement-specific quest props
- universe packs: original space suits, starship interiors, launch bays,
  orbital station modules, alien settlement kits, planet-defense props,
  planetary biome kits, resource harvesters, off-world companions, and
  planet-genesis consoles

Players can purchase, earn, win, craft, remix, or generate assets. Placing,
generating, upgrading, or duplicating powerful assets should cost Signal Credits
so unlimited creativity does not become unlimited spam.

District-native creation spaces are how new assets/actions enter the world:

- Market Walk: clothes, jewelry, shoes, skins, bags, wraps.
- Velocity: vehicles, parts, race routes, garages, takeover events.
- Wondercore: attractions, rides, escape rooms, ticketed experiences.
- Radio Rooftop: tracks, stations, DJ sets, voice shows.
- STATIC PLAY: playable worlds, arena modes, game templates.
- Signal Walk / Channels: venues, fan walls, premieres, channel worlds.
- STATIC Island: yacht parties, island festivals, resort experiences.
- Rise: homes, neighborhood quests, property upgrades.
- Rootside: repairs, black-market tech, hacker missions, street events.
- Sky Crown: lounges, penthouse events, elite memberships.
- Solar Coast: beach clubs, car shows, marine races, coastal events.
- Boardwalk: games, booths, mini attractions, busker stages.
- The Range: ranches, farms, retreats, communes, festivals, compounds.
- Origin Settlements: region-specific blueprints, settlement allies, creature
  companions, robots, mounts, guards, decor, food, clothing, and lore unlocks.
- STATIC Astral Port / Orbital Station: space suits, ship parts, station
  access, alien diplomacy, planet-genesis prompts, off-world companions,
  orbital events, and universe unlock missions.

## Companion Lineage Economy

Creature, pet, mount, robot, mythic, and guardian companions can eventually
support lineage, fusion, incubation, crafting, or lab creation. This is how two
compatible companion assets can produce a new variant with inherited traits.

- Traits can include color, scale, aura, animation set, temperament, rarity,
  mount eligibility, follow behavior, cosmetic effects, and mode-limited
  utility.
- Lineage creation should cost Signal Credits, require cooldowns, or require
  specific in-world facilities such as Entity Labs, mythic nests, machine
  yards, island sanctuaries, or settlement forges.
- Outputs need provenance records so the economy knows which owned assets,
  blueprints, or parent companions created the new item.
- Humanlike Entities do not use breeding systems. Humanlike identity uses
  consent-based legacy, family roles, ancestry, avatar sculpting, or design
  inheritance.
- No lineage result should create a pay-to-win advantage in public spaces. Any
  power must be cosmetic, social, mode-limited, or balanced by district rules.

## Living NPC Economy

STATIC should not treat NPCs as decoration once the Unreal layer exists. NPCs
should become part of a living economy with homes, jobs, needs, routes, memory,
relationships, factions, danger, morale, and time.

- Asset routing decides where each imported character pack belongs before it
  appears in-world.
- Relationship graphs track NPC-to-NPC, NPC-to-player, NPC-to-faction, and
  settlement-to-settlement memory.
- Settlement ticks update population, morale, resources, patrols, jobs, trade,
  threats, festivals, conflict, repairs, and growth.
- AI dialogue can exist only behind lore, cost, moderation, safety, and
  server-authority boundaries.
- Emergent events can create rumors, raids, elections, festivals, migrations,
  boss challenges, surrender offers, treaties, and companion unlocks.
- Economy-critical state cannot be trusted to the client. Land claims,
  recruitment, war outcomes, ownership, credits, rare companions, and settlement
  changes need server authority.

## Companion Evolution Economy

Every companion should support a five-phase evolution arc, from bonded form to
ascended form.

- Phase 1 establishes ownership, name, stance, idle, follow distance, and basic
  visual identity.
- Phase 2 adds awakened visuals, aura, reactions, venue behavior, sound/voice
  layer, and social poses.
- Phase 3 adds mode-limited utility such as scouting, delivery assist,
  guardian behavior, traversal help, mount prep, or land-defense role.
- Phase 4 adds prestige form, rare skins, larger silhouette, special entrance,
  and district status.
- Phase 5 adds ascended form, cinematic summon, unique aura, final title,
  boss/guardian/mount prestige, and high-status recognition.

Evolution should cost some combination of Signal Credits, quest completion,
bonding time, region reputation, settlement trust, lineage/fusion materials,
event participation, or achievement unlocks.

Boss companions can start visually huge or rare, but their Phase 5 ascended
form should still be earned. No companion evolution should become unchecked
public-space power.

## Signal Credits

Signal Credits should be the first internal resource used to rate-limit
generation and building.

- Creating assets costs Signal Credits to prevent infinite spam.
- Higher-value assets cost more Signal Credits.
- Players can earn Signal Credits through play, attendance, creation, jobs,
  events, sales, missions, and reputation systems.
- Early Signal Credits should be treated as non-cash, non-withdrawable game
  currency until legal, tax, payout, anti-fraud, and platform rules are ready.
- Real-money purchases, payouts, marketplace transactions, and creator earnings
  must not launch without payment-provider setup, tax handling, age rules,
  fraud prevention, moderation, terms, and legal review.

## Jobs And Player Income

STATIC can support real jobs and creator income later, but the first versions
should separate in-world fantasy from real financial claims.

Future job paths can include:

- shop owner, mechanic, stylist, DJ, performer, security, builder, architect
- event promoter, team owner, coach, athlete, racer, courier, journalist
- land developer, rancher, farmer, casino/hotel owner, attraction operator
- Gophur, tunnel scout, underground courier, fixer, fence, route broker,
  messenger, package runner, salvage runner, and city-service contractor
- underworld roles, politician, bodyguard, bounty hunter, creator, investor

Real-money earning paths require careful launch controls:

- marketplace seller onboarding
- payout/KYC/tax workflows
- age and jurisdiction rules
- creator rights declarations
- fraud and chargeback handling
- prohibited-content rules
- clear distinction between Signal Credits and real money

## Underground Tunnels And Gophurs

The underground layer gives STATIC a second nervous system beneath the glamour.

- The tunnel network connects different points of STATIC City, including
  Rootside, Market Walk loading zones, Velocity garages, Hidden Walking Street,
  Arena service routes, Radio back corridors, marina freight paths, emergency
  exits, and future secret venues.
- Tunnels are not free teleportation. They require map knowledge, permissions,
  faction reputation, timing, tools, paid help, or story access.
- Some tunnels are safe service corridors. Others can be contested, watched,
  flooded, locked, occupied by NPC crews, or temporarily dangerous during
  events.
- Tunnels support courier jobs, stealth routes, contraband fiction, rescue
  missions, scavenger hunts, hidden clues, asset delivery, black-market repairs,
  and underworld meetings.

**Gophurs** are the working-name underground courier/fixer faction.

- Gophurs are cyberpunk tunnel runners: rough, clever, fast, strange, and hard
  to find. Treat them as a fictional subculture with dignity and style, not a
  joke about homelessness.
- For a price, a Gophur can retrieve an item, deliver a package, move a message,
  scout a route, queue a store pickup, recover a lost asset, or guide a player
  through a tunnel entrance.
- Gophurs create a paid convenience loop: if a player is scared, wanted,
  under-equipped, busy, or too far away, they can pay Signal Credits for a
  courier run instead of going personally.
- Gophur reliability depends on reputation, distance, danger, route knowledge,
  item value, current district heat, and whether the player has a trusted
  Gophur on their team.
- This can become a monetizable and playable system: courier contracts, premium
  delivery passes, crew logistics, black-market jobs, tunnel maps, route
  unlocks, Gophur companions, and asset-store delivery fees.
- Anti-abuse rules are required before launch: no real-world illegal goods, no
  harassment delivery, no bypassing ownership rules, no stolen paid assets
  without approved game mechanics, and no instant duplication of rare items.

## Conflict And Taking Land

The fantasy can include kicking in doors, raids, property defense, and territory
takeovers, but the actual system must be opt-in, rule-bound, and fair.

- Player homes/land should not be griefable by default.
- Vulnerable land states should require opt-in modes, contested windows,
  faction wars, unpaid debt/maintenance systems, wager-free tournaments, or
  specific mission contracts.
- Defensive fantasy is canon: even suburban owners can be dangerous, trained,
  armed in-world, protected by security systems, companions, NPC guards, or
  faction allies.
- Conflict should create consequence, not harassment: heat, law response,
  reputation, prison risk, cooldowns, insurance, recovery paths, and moderation.

## Founder Sovereign Zone

Mr. Stone's private island estate is a special founder-controlled sovereign
zone, separate from public STATIC Island.

- It is not normal contested land.
- Ordinary weapons, raids, and unauthorized combat do not work there.
- Founder security, approved guards, companion systems, and estate defenses
  override normal player conflict rules.
- The island can be visited only through explicit permission, event access,
  story access, or future admin/developer preview states.
- The tone is mythic luxury security: elite original sci-fi guardians, ceremonial
  blade guards, advanced defense systems, aerial/marine perimeter protection,
  and Mr. Stone's three personal companions.
- The island's rare-character ring structure can create high-status discovery,
  but the founder compound remains separate from ordinary island hunts. The
  legendary golden dragon is lore/myth until a real approved asset, rig, and
  encounter system exist.
- This is a fantasy/admin boundary as much as a lore boundary: no griefing the
  founder estate, no forced takeover, no exploit path into protected systems.

## Design Goal

STATIC City is the authored center. The Fields are the player-made expansion.

The player starts with a plot, builds a life, buys or earns assets, expands
territory, joins paths, creates businesses, attends events, sells to others,
defends what they built, and decides whether to live clean, go criminal, build
community, chase status, or try to take over the map.

The promise is not a resettable season board. The promise is persistent world
ownership: build something worth defending.
